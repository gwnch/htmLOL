The Basics

At its most simplistic, propositions are statements that can either be true or false. An example of a propositional statement in English is: If the sun shines, then the birds start singing. This is likely true, unless you have some weird birds in your town. As well as expressing propositions in plain text, they can be translated into mathematical representation.

To mathematically represent the proposition “If the sun shines, then the birds start singing” we first must translate the parts of the proposition into logical variables. Let’s use s and b, for sun and birds respectively. Now we will introduce the symbols that will help us complete the mathematical proposition.

Name Symbol Meaning
Negation ′ or ~ not
Conjunction ∧ and
Disjunction ∨ (inclusive) or
XOR ⊕ (exclusive) either/or
Conditional ⇒ if/then
Biconditional ⇔ if and only if
